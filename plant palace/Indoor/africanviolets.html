African Violets

About

African violet, (genus Saintpaulia), genus of six species of flowering plants in the family Gesneriaceae native to higher elevations in tropical eastern Africa. African violets are common houseplants, especially Saintpaulia ionantha, and can thrive in low light conditions and bloom throughout the year. Hundreds of horticultural varieties have been developed for their various flower colours and shapes, including half-sized miniatures. The members of Saintpaulia are small perennial herbs with thick, hairy, ovate leaves.

Common name:

African violet

Scientific name:

Saintpaulia

Origin Country:

East African country of Tanzania

Watering Cycle:

When it is time to water. African violets should be allowed to dry out between each watering for best results. Overwatering can kill a plant

Light Requirements:

When African violet receive moderate to bright indirect light for 12-16 hours per day
